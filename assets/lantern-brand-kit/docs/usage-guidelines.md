# Lantern Usage Guidelines

## Brand Direction

Lantern should feel calm, reliable, and emergency-ready. Favor dark forest backgrounds, warm gold accents, and restrained use of ember orange for urgency.

## Clear Space

Keep at least the monogram width of padding around the wordmark. Avoid tight placement next to buttons, badges, or icons.

## Backgrounds

Preferred:

- Forest-night solids and dark green gradients
- Warm paper surfaces with low visual noise

Allowed:

- Clean white or near-white layouts with the forest or primary wordmark

Avoid:

- Busy photography
- High-saturation rainbow gradients
- Placing the gold wordmark directly on warm paper without enough contrast

## Wordmark Usage

Use the full wordmark for:

- documentation headers
- repository banners
- splash treatments
- social cards
- marketing or release notes

Use the monogram for:

- favicons
- compact navigation
- launcher icon derivatives
- avatar-sized placements

## App Icon Usage

Use the square icon exports in `icons/` or `favicon/` for any context that requires a filled tile. Use the transparent monogram only when the surrounding surface already supplies the background.

## Accent Discipline

Lantern Gold is the primary accent. Lantern Ember is a secondary alert color, not the default accent. If both appear together, gold should remain dominant.

## Accessibility

- Prefer `lantern-wordmark-white` on dark surfaces.
- Prefer `lantern-wordmark-forest` or `lantern-wordmark-primary` on warm or white surfaces.
- Do not rely on ember orange alone to signal meaning in UI contexts.

## Do Not

- stretch or condense the wordmark
- recolor the monogram with unrelated hues
- place the wordmark over cluttered photography
- recreate the app icon with different corner radii or glow colors
